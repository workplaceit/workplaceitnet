FAQ

Common Questions About Data Cabling in Denver

What is the difference between Cat6 and Cat6A cabling?⌄
Cat6 supports 10GbE up to 55 meters and is suitable for most office environments. Cat6A supports 10GbE up to 100 meters and is recommended for new builds, data centers, or anywhere you need full-distance 10G capability. Cat6A also offers better crosstalk reduction. We recommend Cat6A for any new installation where budget allows — the incremental cost is small, and it future-proofs your infrastructure.
How long does a typical data cabling job take in Denver?⌄
A small office (10-20 drops) typically takes one day. A medium-sized floor (50-100 drops) usually takes 2-3 days. Larger projects with server room build-outs or underground runs are scoped individually. We schedule around your business hours and can do nights or weekends to avoid disruption.
Do you provide a warranty on your cabling work?⌄
Yes. All our data cabling installations are backed by a workmanship warranty. Every run is Fluke DSX tested and certified before we hand off the job, so any issues are caught and fixed before we leave. You also receive the test reports so you have independent documentation of every run's performance.
Can you cable an existing building without tearing up the walls?⌄
In most cases, yes. We use flexible drill bits, cable fishing tools, and surface-mount raceways when in-wall routing is not practical. We do a site survey before quoting to identify the best pathways and give you realistic options for your specific building.
What areas of Denver do you serve?⌄
We serve the entire Denver metro area including Denver, Aurora, Lakewood, Englewood, Littleton, Westminster, Thornton, Arvada, Centennial, Parker, Castle Rock, and surrounding communities along the Front Range.
