What Is Managed IT? A Denver Business Owner's Guide

March 15, 2026 • 6 min read • By Workplace IT

If your IT strategy is still "call someone when it breaks," you're leaving your Denver business exposed — and paying more than you should.

Every week, Denver business owners ask us the same question: "What exactly is managed IT, and do I actually need it?" It's a fair question. The term gets thrown around a lot, but the honest answer depends on your business size, risk tolerance, and how much IT downtime costs you.

The Break-Fix Problem

The traditional model — calling an IT person when something breaks — sounds simple. But there's a hidden cost: every hour your team can't work, access files, or reach customers is revenue walking out the door. For a 10-person Denver company billing $200/hour per employee, a 4-hour outage costs $8,000 in lost productivity. That's before you factor in customer frustration or data risk.

Managed IT flips this model. Instead of reacting to problems, an MSP (Managed Service Provider) monitors your systems 24/7, patches vulnerabilities before they're exploited, and resolves issues — often before you even notice them.

What Managed IT Actually Includes

A quality managed IT contract in Denver typically covers:

  • 24/7 monitoring and alerting — your servers, workstations, and network are watched around the clock
  • Helpdesk support — your team calls or emails when they have an IT issue; a real technician responds fast
  • Patch management — operating system and software updates applied on schedule, not whenever someone remembers
  • Endpoint security — antivirus, EDR (endpoint detection and response), and threat management on every device
  • Backup monitoring — verifying your backups are actually working, not just running
  • vCIO services — strategic IT planning aligned with your business goals

What Does Managed IT Cost in Denver?

Most Denver MSPs charge per user per month. Pricing typically ranges from $75–$150/user/month for a full managed IT package depending on complexity and included services. For a 10-person company, that's roughly $750–$1,500/month — comparable to or less than the salary of a single entry-level IT hire, without the overhead, turnover risk, or coverage gaps.

Do You Need Managed IT?

You probably do if any of these apply:

  • You have 5 or more employees relying on computers to do their jobs
  • You handle sensitive customer data (financial, medical, legal, or government)
  • You've had a virus, ransomware, or data loss incident in the past 3 years
  • Your current IT support is "a guy we call" with no SLA or guaranteed response time
  • You're subject to compliance requirements (CMMC, FINRA, HIPAA, etc.)

If none of those apply and you're a solo operator with minimal IT needs, managed IT might be overkill. But for most Denver businesses with a real team? The math favors the proactive approach every time.

How to Choose the Right MSP in Denver

Not all managed IT providers are created equal. When evaluating options, ask:

  • What's your guaranteed response time for critical issues?
  • Do you have experience with businesses in my industry?
  • What security stack do you use, and why?
  • Can I see a sample of your monthly reporting?
  • What's your offboarding process if I want to leave?

A good MSP should answer all of these without hesitation. If they dodge the security question or can't produce a sample report, keep looking.
